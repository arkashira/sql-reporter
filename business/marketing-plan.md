# Marketing Plan for sql-reporter
## Positioning
### Primary Positioning Statement
"Empowering business users to unlock insights with self-service SQL query capabilities"

### Alternative Positioning Statements
1. "Streamlining data-driven decision-making with automated reporting"
2. "Democratizing access to data insights for non-technical stakeholders"
3. "Revolutionizing business intelligence with intuitive SQL query tools"

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
### Persona 1: Emily Chen, Data Analyst
- Daily Job: Analyzing sales data to inform marketing strategies
- Budget: $10,000 - $50,000 per year for data analysis tools
- Pain Points: Limited access to data, reliance on data engineers for report generation

### Persona 2: David Kim, Business Intelligence Manager
- Daily Job: Developing and maintaining reporting dashboards for executive stakeholders
- Budget: $50,000 - $200,000 per year for business intelligence tools
- Pain Points: High maintenance costs, difficulty scaling reporting capabilities

### Persona 3: Rachel Patel, Operations Manager
- Daily Job: Monitoring key performance indicators (KPIs) to optimize business operations
- Budget: $5,000 - $20,000 per year for data analysis tools
- Pain Points: Limited visibility into operational data, difficulty making data-driven decisions

## Channels
### Top 3 Channels with CAC Estimate
1. **Content Marketing**: Blog posts, case studies, and whitepapers (CAC: $50 - $100)
2. **Social Media**: LinkedIn, Twitter, and Facebook advertising (CAC: $20 - $50)
3. **Email Marketing**: Targeted email campaigns to ICPs (CAC: $10 - $30)

## Content Cadence
### Week-by-Week Content Plan
- Week 1-2: Introduction to sql-reporter, benefits of self-service SQL query capabilities
- Week 3-4: Case studies, success stories from early adopters
- Week 5-6: Technical tutorials, how-to guides for implementing sql-reporter
- Week 7-8: Webinars, workshops on best practices for data analysis
- Week 9-10: Product updates, new feature announcements
- Week 11-12: User-generated content, customer testimonials

## Launch Milestones
### D-30 (30 Days Before Launch)
- Finalize content calendar, social media strategy
- Establish email marketing list, create targeted campaigns

### D-0 (Launch Day)
- Launch sql-reporter, announce to public and ICPs
- Host launch webinar, provide technical support

### D+30 (30 Days After Launch)
- Analyze user adoption, engagement metrics
- Refine content strategy based on user feedback

### D+90 (90 Days After Launch)
- Evaluate success metrics (DAU, $MRR)
- Plan for future product development, expansion of ICPs

## Success Metrics
### DAU (Daily Active Users)
- Target: 100 - 500 users within the first 90 days

### $MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)
- Target: $10,000 - $50,000 within the first 90 days
