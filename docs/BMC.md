# Business Model Canvas (BMC) for sql-reporter

## Value Proposition
### Key Benefits
- **Self-service reporting**: Empower business users to generate reports and insights without relying on data engineers.
- **Easy-to-use interface**: Intuitive platform for non-technical users to create and customize reports.
- **Fast data access**: Quick and seamless access to relevant data for informed decision-making.
- **Scalability**: Handle growing data volumes and user demands without compromising performance.

### Unique Selling Points (USPs)
- **Drag-and-drop report builder**: Simplify report creation with a user-friendly interface.
- **Real-time data refresh**: Ensure reports are always up-to-date and reflect the latest data.
- **Collaboration features**: Enable multiple users to work together on reports and share insights.

## Customer Segments
### Primary Target Market
- **Business users**: Non-technical professionals who require data-driven insights for decision-making.
- **Data analysts**: Technical users who need to create and share reports with stakeholders.

### Secondary Target Market
- **Data engineers**: Technical professionals who can leverage sql-reporter to automate reporting tasks.
- **IT managers**: Leaders responsible for data infrastructure and tooling.

## Channels
### Primary Channels
- **Web application**: sql-reporter's self-service platform accessible via web browsers.
- **API**: Programmatic access for developers to integrate sql-reporter with other tools.

### Secondary Channels
- **Documentation and tutorials**: Online resources for users to learn and get started with sql-reporter.
- **Customer support**: Email, chat, and phone support for users with questions or issues.

## Revenue Streams
### Primary Revenue Streams
- **Subscription-based model**: Offer tiered pricing for users based on features and data volume.
- **Custom implementation**: Provide on-site or remote implementation services for large-scale deployments.

### Secondary Revenue Streams
- **Training and consulting**: Offer workshops, training sessions, and consulting services for users.
- **Data integration services**: Assist users in integrating sql-reporter with their existing data infrastructure.

## Cost Structure
### Fixed Costs
- **Development and maintenance**: Ongoing investment in software development, testing, and maintenance.
- **Infrastructure and hosting**: Costs associated with running and scaling sql-reporter's infrastructure.

### Variable Costs
- **Marketing and advertising**: Expenses related to promoting sql-reporter and attracting new users.
- **Customer support and success**: Costs associated with providing support and ensuring user satisfaction.

## Key Resources
### Human Resources
- **Development team**: Software engineers, data engineers, and quality assurance engineers.
- **Customer support team**: Support engineers, success managers, and account managers.

### Physical Resources
- **Infrastructure and hosting**: Servers, storage, and networking equipment.
- **Software and tools**: Development tools, testing frameworks, and collaboration software.

## Key Activities
### Primary Activities
- **Software development**: Continuously improve and expand sql-reporter's features and capabilities.
- **Customer support and success**: Ensure users are satisfied and successful with sql-reporter.

### Secondary Activities
- **Marketing and advertising**: Promote sql-reporter and attract new users.
- **Business development**: Explore new revenue streams and partnerships.

## Key Partners
### Primary Partners
- **Data providers**: Companies that provide data for sql-reporter to integrate with.
- **Technology partners**: Companies that offer complementary tools and services.

### Secondary Partners
- **System integrators**: Companies that help integrate sql-reporter with existing systems.
- **Consulting firms**: Firms that provide strategic guidance and implementation services.
